City Mend
Introduction

North End Park in Boston, Massachusetts serves as a vital link for much of northeastern boston and allows for interconnectivity through many aspects of the city. However, the end of this strip is abruptly cut off by the rising of the highway underneath it, leaving much to be desired for the conclusion of such an essential park and urban connector.

This competition entry seeks to mend the park's many links through a network of paths that create different atmospheres and elevations in response to the queues received from the city fabric. Boston, known for its culture, commerce, and tourist appeal, deserves a space malleable enough to allow for all these different types of events, which often involve very different densities of population.

With this in mind, the site is split with monuments and pin points that attract the user and define the center of each, smaller space. This allows for more personalized activities, though these spaces can quickly be unified when needed, fitting for the density of a city with many varied activities.
