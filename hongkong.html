Interim Urbanism
Introduction

Collaborator: Laura Davidson

Hong Kong is victim to a history of being stifled and manipulated by super powers of the world. This has affected the architecture of the country in a way that primarily gives agency to private developers with an international public in mind. Kowloon's plethora of generic top-down skyscrapers casts a large shadow on the local identity of the Hong Kongian, but, how can these wildly different aspirations for this international nation be resolved?

In this system, we take the now abandoned Kai Tak Airport east of Kowloon and create the infrastructure to build a new city fabric that will force these opposing publics to face one another. This system is composed of a series of thin, sometimes rotating, wall panels of different materials that provide necessary infrastructure such as electricity and water. The materials of the panels serve as deciding factors for what a private developer can do with them and what a local Hong Kongian can transform them into.

We aspire to see a new fabric where the international and local public can face each other and understand the culture of their supposed opposite, and perhaps, maybe even collaborate in creating a city fabric that incorporates both their desires for a nation shared by the world.
